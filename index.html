Pursu keeps track of all your applications, progress, and relevant information simply based on your incoming emails and requires minimal user input. We are currently in the closed beta phase, but look out for more information from me later in August!

  • Winter 2019
    EECS 281: Data Structures and Algorithms (4)

    This course may be notorious for its time-consuming projects and difficult exams, but I believe it should really be notable for its interesting and extremely practical content. I can say with certainty that this is my favorite class that I’ve taken to date. Not only did I learn about various data structures and algorithms that are practically applicable (like the course title suggests), but I also enhanced my problem-solving skills and was challenged to apply myself throughout the course.

    EECS 201: Computer Science Pragmatics (1)

    I strongly believe that anybody interested in software development should take this course. I learned so many tips and tricks about text editors, operating systems, and command line interfaces that I thought were inconsequential at the time, but have proven to be immensely useful when working on side projects and during internships.

    PSYCH 111: Introduction to Psychology (4)

    This was the only non-technical course I took during the semester, and it also ended up being one of the most interesting courses I have taken to date. I’ve always been fascinated with the human brain and what drives decision making, and this class helped satiate my curiosity. It also introduced me to a subject that regardless of my degree requirements, I am very interested in learning more about.

    IOE 265: Probability and Statistics (3)

    I registered for this course out of mere chance: the other classes that fulfill my degree’s STATS requirements were already at capacity. Despite this stressful scheduling start and the unforeseen impact COVID-19 had on this semester, I learned a lot about the fields of probability and statistics and got full value out of this class. I recommend this course to anyone who needs to fulfill a requirement.

  • Fall 2019
    EECS 203: Discrete Mathematics (4)

    As someone who was always interested in math but was most recently familiar with taking calculus classes, this class was a great introduction to “a different side” of mathematics. One of the most valuable things I learned was a standard for proof-based mathematics that has inspired me to pursue a Mathematics minor. The emphasis on logical reasoning and problem solving methods instilled in me by this class have also pervaded many different aspects of my education as well as my life.

    EECS 280: Programming and Introductory Data Structures (4)

    My first semester of college marked the beginning of a rule that allowed freshmen to take this class in their first semester. Seeing that I fulfilled the requirements and had previous experience coding in C++, I snatched up the opportunity and didn’t look back. I thoroughly enjoyed learning about basic data structures and algorithms and being able to experience the structure of coding classes at Michigan so early on set a stable foundation for all future classes I would take.

    ECON 101: Principles of Economics (4)

    This is a class that I believe everyone should take. Regardless of major, learning the basic economic principles that guide the society we live in is extremely important to everyone. This class instilled in me the idea that learning about different subjects not necessarily applicable to your major can be valuable.
